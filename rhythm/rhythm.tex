%For instructor only
Explain that just like with fractions, a whole can represent a different thing and how much is $\frac{1}{2}$ or $\frac{1}{4}$ of it, depends on the whole. 

Moreover, even though equivalent fractions, such as $\frac{1}{2}$ or $\frac{2}{4}$ might represent the same amount, in one case the whole is split only in two pieces, whereas on the other, the whole the pieces are smaller, since each whole is split into four.

You can use a cake or pizza analogy. A quarter slice might be the same as two eight slices, but in one case you have a single slice and in the other you have two smaller slices. 

With music is the same a whole note can be divided only in $2$ beats, or perhaps in $4$ or $8$. Students can think about beats as claps.
\end{instructorNotes}

\begin{question} Notes and rests are organized into small sections called
  \textbf{measures} or \textbf{bars}. Most of the time, and unless otherwise indicated, all the measures in a music piece have the same length.
  The \textbf{time signature} tell us how much music fits in a measure.
\begin{center}
 \begin{tikzpicture}
  \node at (0,0) {\meterfrac{\raisebox{.2em}{M}}{W}};
  \node[anchor=west] at (1,1) {number of beats in a measure};
  \node[anchor=west] at (1,-1) {number of beats in a whole note};
  \draw[->] (1,.9) -- (.4,.4);
  \draw[->] (1,-.9) -- (.4,-.4);
 \end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}
  For example in the passage below, the time signature is
  $\timesig{3}{4}$:
  
  \begin{center}
    \includegraphics[width=.8\textwidth]{menuett34.png}
  \end{center}
  Since the time signature is $\timesig{3}{4}$, a whole-note is split into $4$ beats, but each measure only uses three beats. Hence, there are three quarter-notes, or equivalent, per measure.

Here are some examples of music in common time signatures. Listen to them carefully, trying to catch the time signature.
  \begin{itemize}
 \item $\timesig{4}{4}$
 \item $\timesig{3}{4}$
 \item $\timesig{6}{8}$
 \item $\timesig{5}{4}$
   \end{itemize}

    \item Find another example of any of those time signatures.
    \end{enumerate}
\end{question}
